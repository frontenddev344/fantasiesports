5 Must-Know Tips for Fantasy Football Drafts

Preparing for your Fantasy NFL draft can make all the difference. Here are five tips to ensure you build a competitive team:

  • Focus on Running Backs Running backs often score the most points and are crucial to a successful fantasy team. Aim to secure top-tier running backs early in the draft to build a strong foundation. Their ability to contribute both rushing yards and receptions makes them highly valuable.
  • Watch for Bye Weeks Bye weeks are a part of the NFL season and can impact your fantasy lineup. Be aware of when your players have their bye weeks and avoid drafting too many players from the same week. Planning ahead will help you avoid having too many key players absent in one week.
  • Consider Flexibility Look for players who offer flexibility and can perform well in multiple positions. Versatile players who can be used in different roles or have dual eligibility can provide valuable depth and adaptability to your roster.
  • Don’t Overvalue Quarterbacks While quarterbacks are important, they are also plentiful. Focus on other key positions early in the draft, such as running backs and wide receivers. Quality quarterbacks can often be found in the middle rounds, allowing you to strengthen other areas of your team first.
  • Stay Updated on Injuries Injuries can impact player performance and availability. Keep track of pre-season injury reports and updates to avoid drafting players who are dealing with significant injuries or are at risk of missing games.
